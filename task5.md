## Case Study: Phishing

Phishing is a cybercrime where attackers pretend to be a trusted organization to trick people into sharing sensitive information like passwords, bank details, or personal data. These fake messages usually come through email, text messages, or social media and often look very real.

A phishing attack usually happens in a few steps. First, the attacker sends a message that looks like it is from a trusted source, such as a bank or college. The message often creates urgency, for example by saying the account will be blocked if no action is taken. Next, the message includes a link that takes the victim to a fake website. This website looks similar to the real one. When the victim enters their login details, the attacker collects this information. Finally, the attacker uses the stolen data for fraud or unauthorized access.
